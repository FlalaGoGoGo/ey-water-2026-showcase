# LinkedIn Post Draft

This was our team's first machine learning competition, and we wanted to treat it like a real end-to-end ML project rather than a single model submission.

We joined the **2026 EY AI & Data Challenge: Optimizing Clean Water Supply**, where the goal was to predict water quality indicators across river locations in South Africa.

Over the course of the project, we built a full experimentation workflow around:

- target-wise modeling for three different water quality variables
- feature engineering with Landsat and TerraClimate data
- external signal exploration with hydro, terrain, soil, rainfall, weather, and power-climate sources
- calibration, fallback logic, and failure reviews after weak leaderboard rounds
- structured round tracking so each submission taught us something

Our best tracked official score reached **0.376**, but more importantly, this project taught us how to think about ML experimentation: not just model training, but validation discipline, post-processing, error control, and learning from failure.

We turned the project into a public case-study page with visuals, milestones, and selected notebooks:

`https://flalagogogo.github.io/ey-water-2026-showcase/`

And we cleaned up the supporting code and materials into a public repository:

`https://github.com/FlalaGoGoGo/ey-water-2026-showcase`

If you are also learning applied ML through projects, competitions, or messy real-world data, I would love to hear what helped you most.
